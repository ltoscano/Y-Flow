Customers and stakeholders of the private company of Fly 540 Kenya may get the opportunity to invest if that company will be listed in Nairobi city, NSE, next month.
This is after Fly 540 Kenya, announcing that it had collected a total of 194 million from the sales of its shares to investors, which is equivalent to 158 percent of shareholders who were registered, most of them being investors from here in the country.
According to the report read by the journalists yesterday by the chairman of Flightlink company, George Simbachawene, during the occasion of announcing those results, the board announced the process that it has been given permission from NSE (GS), which is the first step, to be postponed for registration.
