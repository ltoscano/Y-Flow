Inspector general of police (IGP), Ernest Mangu, will be handed the chairmanship of Confederation of regional police in Southern Africa (SARPCCO) at a meeting that will take place on September 16th this year, in Arusha town.

The chairmanship will be handed over to IGP Mangu by head of the police force Mozambique where he will hold that position for a period of one year.
The report of the spokesman of the police force in the country, Advera Kisimba, that meeting will be held on September 14th and 15 this year, in order to prepare a agenda and recommendations that will be opened by the Vice President, Samia Suluhu Hassan.
"In the meeting the various issues to deal with crime will be discussed, including crime, crossing the borders, together with putting strategies and resolutions of working together with the countries that form the federation," said Jamala and added.
At the same time, the meeting will discuss about the implementation of various resolutions through the executive committee of directors of investigation, the executive committee of the law, the executive committee of the training and the executive committee of the women's security network.
SARPCCO was established in 1995, with the aim of strengthening the cooperation of the police force in the Southern African countries in dealing with crime.
The union is being created by 15 countries which are Tanzania, Angola, Botswana, Malawi, Lesotho, Mauritius, the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), Mozambique, Namibia, South Africa, Zimbabwe, Swaziland, Madagascar.
