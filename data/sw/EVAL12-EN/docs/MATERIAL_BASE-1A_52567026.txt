
The council of ministers donated a pipeline to oil.

Has been written by our correspondent.

It has been published today, September 2016.

The government has officially launched the implementation of the project of construction of crude oil pipeline from Kabambale in Makinga district, Uganda to the port of Tanga, in the country.

The decision was reached in a session of the ministers that took place yesterday at State House, Dar es Salaam under the chairmanship of President John Magufuli.
The report of that step by the government to donate the construction of that project was given by the Prime Minister Kassim Majaliwa in that meeting.
Majaliwa said the construction of that project which will start at any time from now, is expected to be completed in the year 2020.
According to that report, the construction of that pipeline is expected to cost 500 billion American dollars and among them three billion dollars will be used to build a pipeline from Tanzania.
The big project will be implemented in partnership with construction and operations by involving the Total company of France, the CNOOC Company, we are in England and two governments of Tanzania and Uganda.
Majaliwa said that the pipeline which is 1,443 kilometers is expected to transport crude oil 200,000 shillings per day and every month will be given a lot in American Dollars by 12,02.
