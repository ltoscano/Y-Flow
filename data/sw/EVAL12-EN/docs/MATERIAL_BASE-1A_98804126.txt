28 people are going on in the fighting in Congo.

27th September, 2016

More than 28 people have been killed during four days of attacks between security forces and militia in the Democratic Republic of Congo.
According to the Minister of Internal Affairs in the country, Evarista A, among those involved are four security officers, 14 soldiers and six citizens including three other children, while several others were arrested.
The fights broke out last Thursday when the militants brought in Kasanga city to revenge against the killing of their leader Kamnada Mkumbo who was killed by police on the month of August.
A group of those fighters that are fighting against what they used as the government to control power in the central region of Kasangati in that country.
