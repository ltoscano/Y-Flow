The representatives of the Human Rights Commission of East Africa, have met Dar es Salaam for looking at various cases of conflicts since the year 2013 and realizing that many have been focused on issues of land and administration.

The chairperson of the commission of human rights and good governance in the country, Bahame Nyanduga said, the representatives met for three days and identifying those cases of conflicts and agreed to work together in order to solve the situation.
He said they found many conflicts there are land or land resources like minerals, oil, gas, use of water, forests, breeders and farmers, limits, investors and citizens together with the use of human land and wildlife.
