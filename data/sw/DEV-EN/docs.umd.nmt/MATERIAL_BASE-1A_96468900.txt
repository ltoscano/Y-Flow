A three-day meeting that includes experts of the public universities of East Africa and chief executives of the private sector, is expected to focus more about the shortage of experts and needs of employment market.
That meeting of the forum of Wasomi and private sectors and the exhibition of the year 2013 that will take place at the Jomo International Conference Center Kenyatta Kenya, will involve stakeholders from five countries members of the East African Community (EAC).
Those countries include Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda and Burundi.
That meeting that was under the theme, "to unite the scholars and the private sector through the public sector," unawbrings together experts who are experienced in different sectors and senior businessmen, they will discuss issues that touch all sides of the participants of that meeting.
The executive director of the East African Trade Council (EABC), Andrew Luzze told the Independent East African Information Organization (EANA) that the private sector of universities will discuss together with the strategy of handling the issue of unemployment and lack of skills that is required to the graduates of universities in that region of East Africa.
"Students say there is no kazi.private sector says they have a job, but the skills that is required and the private sector is not the one who the relevant graduates have," he said.
Speaking to the journalist, the Executive Secretary of the Council of Universities in East Africa, Professor Mayunga Mkunof has agreed with those views.
The problem said, it is in all five countries members of EAC. "We want to discuss how to make adjustments of the teaching curriculum which will enable them to produce graduates with skills and direction."
Luzze said about 250 participants have already confirmed to attend.
