
Majaliwa looking for the commemoration of the disabled nationally nationally.

It was written by Sifa Lubasi, Dodoma, Dodoma.

It has been published: 1st December 2016.

The commemoration of the day of people with disabilities in the world, which nationally were expected to take place on December 3, this year in Dar es Salaam, have been postponed and now every province will commemorate its individually to reduce costs.

While giving a statement of being postponed for the commemorations, the minister of state, the office of the president, regional administration and local government (Tamisemi), George Simbachawene, has said the request of the district councils are to transport members of people with disabilities so that they do that commemoration in Dar es Salaam city.
He said the instructions that were given by the prime minister, Kassim Majaliwa for the heads of provinces is that instead of the national commemorations to collect people with all disabilities in the provinces to go to Dar es Salaam to do in their regions.
"I take this opportunity to command the heads of provinces and heads of districts and directors to make sure that they provide enough cooperation of these parties so as to achieve those commemorations," said Simbachawene.
He has directed the Provincial Commissioner of Dar es Salaam, Paul Makonda, he ensures that in the area they had planned to make the activity by giving them a donation that will deserve.
Simbachawene has said that the situation will help reduce the costs instead of all they would be transported from the provinces and go to Dar es Salaam and now that we are doing it in the provinces and district.
