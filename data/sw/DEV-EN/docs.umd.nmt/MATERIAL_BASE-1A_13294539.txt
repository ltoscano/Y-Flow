
Ireland is value of Tanzania 8 higher education on top of the top

It was written by Basil Basil.

It has been published: 30 September 2016.

Four professionals have been given an opportunity for funding and the Embassy of Ireland to study a masters degree in different professions.

The funding has been given through a special program of the government of that country to fund the studies and the training of the Irish Aid Programme that is supervised by the government of Ireland.
Ambassador of Ireland in the country, Paul Sherlock, speaking at an event to announce the funding at the embassy of that country, Dar es Salaam, congratulated them who got the opportunity.
"I salute each one of you for getting this opportunity.
Since the introduction of this program 40 years ago, we have helped of Tanzania more than 200 participating in the program of Irish Aid program Programme, "said Ambassador Sherlock.
He added that the program is important for the relationship between Tanzania and Ireland and the training were started to help be achieved with the goals set by the government of Tanzania in its second phase of development, which is being implemented for five years.
Areas where the beneficiaries of the funding will be studying in their degree degree is sustainable farming, nutrition and managing and evaluate health and they will be studying at the University of Dar es Salaam (UDSM), the University of Sokoine (SUA), University of Dodoma (UDOM) and the University Mzumbe.
He said the beneficiaries are the professionals who agreed to focus on their professional area and who are finishing the studies they will be ready to work with their institutions and use skills who will get to help the entire community.
Yesterday also of Tanzania, 10 were given an identity certificates to finish a degree degree this year through the program, including two who got scholarships to study in Ireland.
