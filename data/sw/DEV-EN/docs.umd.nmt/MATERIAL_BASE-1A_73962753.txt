Some of the pregnant women with albinism living in the villages and those who live apart from the clinic or health centers have been stuck to get important services of the clinic for the mother's service and child.
The coordinator of the mirror of Ilagala village, Uvin in the province, Edward Simon has requested all councils in that province to put a procedure that will enable people with albinism to get a clinic service.
Speaking during a special seminar for building capacity to the community about the war against stigma and gender violence, Simon said some of the pregnant women with albinism did not attend the clinic because there is no better environment and conducive for them.
"Some live in the villages apart from the areas with the service of the clinic and they don't have reliable transportation and they can't walk on foot for fear of their lives due to the misfaith about this community group," said Simon.
The coordinator wanted the government to make a good vote in the villages for the group and others in the community so that they can be served as required.
A resident of Msimba village, Mungonya Said Bilmark who also has albinism said that the plan is implemented in encouraging women with that state to attend the numbers to the clinic in order to get health care.
The chairperson of Katubuka estate in Kigoma, Shedrack Bulushi said together with urging the government through its councils to put a better environment for people with special needs about the issue of health, also the community is supposed to help the group to get important needs.
Also, Bulushi said that stigma has been contributing some of those with albinism non-compliance with health services are found.
