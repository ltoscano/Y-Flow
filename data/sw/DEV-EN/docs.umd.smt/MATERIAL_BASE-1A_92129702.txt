
Yachangia Arusha development company

It has been written by John Mhala, Arusha

Imechapishwa: 15 November 2016

The company Dharam Singh Hanspaul company that makes the vehicles of the tourism board here in the city, has allocated millions of money for the purpose of various development activities of the residents of the city and its suburbs.

It is said that that is one of the ways of supporting the efforts of the government of the fifth phase of caring for the low level citizens.
Speaking at the launch of a bridge linking the ward and Moshono locations Engutoto in Arusha that was built by the company at a cost of more than 67 million shillings, the owner of the company, Hans Paul said his company is hand in hand with the government in Arusha province for caring for the low level citizens.
Paul said that bridge is a savior and a donation to the many people who were watching the great discomfort to cross the especially during the rainy season due to lack of reliable and saw kilichokuwepo previously kiliashiria the state of a high risk to children, the elderly, motorcycle riders, pedestrian and there is no vehicle that was all over and the ability to pass there.
He said apart from the bridge, also the company has three classrooms in Engutoto Ward at a cost of more than 50 million shillings, which is one of the ways of wanting to students learning while in class and to sit at the desks.
The owner said that together with the building and to provide assistance, also the company has employed more than 500 local employees in three anazomiliki, different company is one of the ways of solving the problem of employment here in Arusha region and the country in general.
