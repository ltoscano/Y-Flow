
The government solve the challenges of the health sector

It has been written by Hellen Mlacky

Imechapishwa: 29th November 2016

The deputy minister of health, community development, gender, the elderly and children, Doctor Hamis Kigwangalla has said the government is required to prepare to deal with the biggest challenge in the health sector resulting from the increase in people where every year, more than one million children are born.

Doctor Kigwangalla made the remarks yesterday in the debate of the system of providing health services in the country that was organized by the embassy of Switzerland in the country as part of the commemoration of 50 years of cooperation between the government and Tanzania.
"There are a large number of people increases every year where Tanzania tunapokea every year, more than one million children, therefore, Tanzania in the next 50 years we will have more than 100 million people and this is a very big challenges in the health sector, health and our economy," said the deputy minister and increase: "Must tujiandae on the issues of the environment and the way to get food, employment and on how to serve our people in health and other sectors, therefore every day we see zinaongezeka challenges in the health sector and the budget increases but still ukaona no improvements due to various challenges".
Moreover, he said they have received opinions about the cost of enrolling them in the health insurance system to cater for forgiveness groups that have been given in accordance with the policies, the elderly, children under the age of five years, the pregnant women and diseases that hayatozwi services.
