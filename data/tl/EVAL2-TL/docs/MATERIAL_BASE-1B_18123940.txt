Pagtatatag ng joint crisis management team para sa OFWs, hinirit
By Armida Rico July 16, 2016
Hinimok kahapon ni ACTS-OFW Partylist Rep. John Bertiz III ang pamunuan ng Department of Foreign Affairs (DFA) at Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) na magtalaga ng isang Joint Crisis Management Team para sa mga overseas Filipino workers (OFWs).
Ito ay dahil sa problemang kinakaharap ng mga nasabing empleyado na sunud-sunod nang tinatanggal sa kanilang mga trabaho sa Middle East, partikular sa Saudi Arabia.
Ayon kay Bertiz, ang pagpasa ng House Resolution (HR) 13 ay ukol sa pagrerekomenda ng isang Joint Crisis Management Team sa pangunguna ng DFA at DOLE upang maasikaso ang libu-libong OFWs na nawalan ng hanapbuhay matapos ma-bankrupt ang ilang mga kumpanya sa Saudi Arabia at iba pang lugar sa Middle East. “The apparent lack of coordination and policy consensus between the two departments continue to be felt on the ground by affected OFWs across Saudi Arabia and in some other areas across the Middle East, leading their families at home and workers onsite to the edge of desperation, if not depression,” nakasaad sa resolusyon.
Pahayag pa ni Bertiz, bilang isang dating OFW ang hirap na dinadanas ng mga manggagawang Pinoy kasabay ng pagbagsak ng ilang major trading and construction companies sa Saudi, tulad ng Saudi Oger Ltd., Mohammad Al-Mojil Group, Mohammed Al Barghash Trading and Contracting Co.
“Every effort must be undertaken by Philippine diplomatic offices in the Middle East and in some other parts of the world to ensure that OFWs fighting for their legal rights to full compliance be successfully pursued in the name of labor justice and human rights,” ani pa ni Bertiz.
Ayon sa mga natanggap na report ng partylist, libu-libong OFWs ang apektado sa pagbagsak ng ekonomiya sa Middle East na dulot ng terorismo, digmaan at paglubog ng presyo ng langis sa global market.
“Our OFWs remain stranded in work camps and accommodation awaiting the payment of back wages and end-of-service benefits for more than a year, with some workers suffering inhumane conditions,” ani Bertiz.
Sa huli ay mariing inirekomenda ng baguhang mambabatas ang Joint Crisis Management Team na tututok sa kalagayan ng mga OFWs.
“The two departments must waste no time in reaching to laid-off workers. Due recognition and urgent assistance must also be provided to workers laid off or retrenched due to redundancy of positions,” dagdag niya.
