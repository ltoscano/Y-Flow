SEAG gold asinta ng Perlas Pilipinas
By Ramil Cruz October 12, 2016 Asinta naman ng Pilipinas ang unang gold medal sa 29th Southeast Asian Games 2017 sa Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia sa darating na Agosto 19-31, makalipas ang pagwalis sa anim na laro pamedalyang ginto sa 9th Southeast Asian Basketball Association Women’s Championship nu’ng Setyembre 20-26, 2016 sa nasabi ring bansa.
“The target now is the Southeast Asian Games. If we win the gold in the SEA Games, then it will be our ticket to the (18th Jakarta, Indonesia) Asian Games (2018),” sambit ni Perlas Pilipinas coach Patrick Aquino.
Suportado ni Dioseldo Sy ng Blackwater, hindi pa nakaka-gold ang Filipina cagers sa SEAG habang may pitong silver at limang bronze medals na ito sa biennial meet. Pero para matapos na ang matagal na tagtuyot ng Pinay dribblers sa SEAG gold, giniit ni Aquino sa weekly PSA Forum kahapon sa Shakey’s Malate, Manila na kailangang palakasin pa ang Nationals ng Fil-foreigners o naturalized player.
“We need tall players so we can match up especially in the Asian level where Japan is now the number one team. But if you look at China, it has players as tall as 6’7” or 6’8” or forwards at 6’4” and guards standing six feet,” aniya pa.
