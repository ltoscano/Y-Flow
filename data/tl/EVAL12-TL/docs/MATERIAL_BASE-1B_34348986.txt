Blackwater iiwas, 3 pa hahabol sa quarters By Ramil Cruz SSeptember 7, 2016
Iwas-karit ni kamatayan ang Blackwater samantalang aakyat pa ang tatlong mga nasa lower portion para paigtingin ang mga paghahabol sa apat pang silya sa quarterfinals sa paselang nasa homestretch ng PBA Governors Cup elims.
Kaboksing ng kulelat na Blackwater (1-7) ang nasa sixth spot na Phoenix (4-4) sa 4:15 p.m. at alas-siyete ng gabi ang derby ng magkatali sa ninth spot na Alaska (3-5) at GlobalPort (3-5) sa Ynares Center-Antipolo. Dapat na ma-sweep ng Elite ang last three games, kasama GP at defending champion San Miguel Beer para magkatsansa pa sa next round via playoff game. Pero may kahirapan, lalo’t nasa six losing skid ang Elite pinakabago sa mga kamay ng Alaska 107-87.
Pumapakay pa ang kasagupaang PP ng third straight wins. Huling pinaliguan ng Fuel Masters ang Star nu’ng Biyernes, 106-93.
Balak namang malagok ng Alaska ang unang winning streak tapos hakbangan ang Elite sa laban sa Batang Pier na babawi sa 122-120 defeat sa TNT KaTropa dalawang araw pa lang ang nakararaan.
“GlobalPort is one of the most explosive teams in the league. Honestly it feels like you have to worry about three imports on one team with the way Stanley (Pringle) and Terrence (Romeo) are playing. Coach Franz (Pumaren) is in my opinion one of the the premier coaches in the country, so it is no surprise that this is such a dangerous team,” wika ni Aces coach Alex Compton.
“You don’t stop a team like GlobalPort with only individual defensive effort, no matter how crucial that is in this game. Everyone who steps on the floor for us will have to be ready to play team defense and hustle,” hirit pa niya.
