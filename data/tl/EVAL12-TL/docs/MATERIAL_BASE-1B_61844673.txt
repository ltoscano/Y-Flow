80 teams labu-labo sa baseball/softball By Ramil Cruz November 16, 2016
Paglalabu-labuhan ng 80 teams ng 25 schools sa Metro Manila at nearby provinces ang 10 titles sa 2nd Philippine Tot Baseball-Metro Schools League Baseball and Softball Tournaments na magbubukas sa Nobyembre 20 sa Sto. Niño baseball field sa Marikina City.
Inihayag ni PTBF president Rodolfo Tingson, na dahil sa laki ng bilang ng mga kalahok sa tuwing Linggo lang na mga labanan, aabutin ang mga kumpetisyon hanggang sa Marso 2017.
“When we started this tournament more than 20 years ago we had only two schools represented in a dual meet. Now we have 80 teams from 25 schools,” namutawi pa sa kanya kahapon sa Philippine Sportswriters Association (PSA) Forum sa Shakey’s Pizza Parlor sa Malate, Manila. Isa na ito na biggest school-based at community-based baseball and softball tourneys sa bansa na mga papadrinuhan ng HMR Trading Haus at Loro Sports, ayon naman kay Philippine women’s rugby team member Aida Milby.
Ang mga koronang nakataya ay Shetland (3-6 years old tee-ball), Pinto (7-9 pitched coached), Mustang (9-10), Bronco (11-12), Pony (13-14), Colt (15-18), elementary girls’ softball (9-13), high school girls’ softball (14-18) at General Championship sa boy’s baseball at sa girls’ softball.
Ang mga kasali ay ang Ateneo de Manila University, La Salle Greenhills School, International School Manila, Parents for Education, Inc. Southridge School, La Salle College Antipolo, Marist College, De La Salle Lipa, Manila Japanese Baseball, De La Salle Canlubang, PAREF Woodrose School; Miriam College, Saint Pedro Poveda College, PAREF Rosehill School, College of the Holy Spirit, St. Paul College Pasig, Assumption High School Antipolo, Rizal High School, Quezon City High School, at Antipolo, Muntinlupa, Tanauan, Marikina, Cainta, Pasig, at Quezon City team.
