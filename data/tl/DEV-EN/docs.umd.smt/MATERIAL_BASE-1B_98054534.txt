Vice President Leni struggle in Land conversion ban By Bernard Taguinod October 15, 2016 averted by Vice President Leni Robredo the plan of the Executive department that the implementation of the ban on the Land conversion in the span of two years because this is a big effect on the hard work of the government to give the house the citizens to not have their own house and lot.
In the petition, signed by Robredo, chairperson of the Housing and Urban Development Coordinating Council (HUDCC), he wished that the release of the irekonsidera "blanket" Executive Order (EO) magpapatigil in Land conversion in the span of two years.
Aside from the HUDCC, also included in the petitioner the Department of Finance (DOF), Department of Budget and Management (DBM) and the National Economic Development Authority (NEDA).
First proposed by the militant group in the legislative chamber the ban on the Land conversion to ensure that was not convert Industrial in the agricultural Land.
However, because the EO "blanket" Robredo, are worried that even the land planned construction of the housing will be affected, so that is why it is made the Housing project of the government.
"The proposed blanket two-Year ban on Land use conversion will introduce further delays in the Housing and resettlement processes, which will exacerbate the insurmountable Housing backlog that we Are working to address," according to Robredo.
Primary will be affected are the housing projects in the Yolanda-affected area because the areas in Eastern Visayas region are all inside the agricultural zones.
