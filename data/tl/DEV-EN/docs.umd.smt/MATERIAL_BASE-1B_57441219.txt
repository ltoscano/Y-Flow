
Pangasinan Health Office warned the disease leptospirosis

August 24, 2016 April M. Montes

Pangasinan, August 24 (Pia) - once again reminded of the Provincial Health Office (PHO) the citizens in Pangasinan double your caution against the disease leptospirosis, especially during the rainy season.

The leptospirosis is an illness to rise from pagkakababad of the feet, especially those with wounds, the flood water that kontaminado of the rats and other animals.

Most common symptom of this illness is having a high fever and flu, namumulang eyes, naninilaw skin, abuse of the rear part of the leg and color tsaa to pee.

Advised by Doctor Anna Maria Teresa De Guzman, provincial health officer, the people to avoid went down the flood water to avoid sickness.

"If this cannot be avoided, they can use bota lulusong when the flood water," harvest De Guzman.

He said, the designated express lane in the hospital in the province for the nilalagnat of three to five days to detect if affected by the leptospirosis immediately to the patient.

Meanwhile, under the watch of leptospirosis 17 towns and cities in Pangasinan.

According to PHO, including the following: Mangaldan (10 cases of leptospirosis were recorded); Sta.
Barbara (three were recorded cases of leptospirosis); Aguilar, Balungao, Rosales, San Jacinto and Sual (there were two recorded cases); and Alaminos City, Asingan, Bani, Bayambang, Lingayen, Manaoag, Pozorrubio, San Manuel, San Nicolas and Tayug (each have a cases of leptospirosis recorded).

The PHO added that there are four victims of leptospirosis were recorded dead.

These are from the town of San Nicolas, Tayug, Manaoag and Mangaldan.

(JNPD / AMM / Pia-1, Pangasinan)
