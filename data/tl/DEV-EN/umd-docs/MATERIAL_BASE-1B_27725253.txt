Pinoys in Sokor cautious in MERS-CoV

Malou's Escudero (Pilipino Star Ngayon) | Updated June 7, 2015 - 12: 00am

Manila, Philippines - yesterday advised the Filipinos who lives in South Korea to be careful to avoid the Middle East Respiratory Syndrome coronavirus (MERS-CoV) after confirm the cases of the said disease.
According to Deputy Presidential Spokesperson Abigail Valte said, in the 55,000 Filipinos are living in South Korea.
The three died and 36 were infected based on the latest data of the World Health Organization (WHO) of South Korean Health ministry, CGI.
Valte believes that the Filipinos there will be safe if they follow the directive of the government of South Korea.
1,500 people who allegedly ordered to self quarantine and in the 1,000 school were closed down to prevent the spread of the virus.
