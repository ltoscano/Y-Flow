No ceasefire - AFP.

Joy Cantos (Filipino Star Ngayon) It will be recalled updated SSetyembre 15, 2013 - 12: 00am.

Manila, Philippines - there is no ceasefire, as long as the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF) breakaway group against the troops of the government and basic instalation of the government in Zamboanga City are continuously firing.
This was emphasized yesterday by Defense Secretary Voltaire Gazmin after Vice President Jejomar Binay announced that the Secretary and MNLF founding chairman Nur Misuari has agreed to implement a peaceful solution to the crisis in Zamboanga City which is on the seventh day now.
Gazmin said that he called on Friday night of Binay and said that he could have a dialogue with Misuari.
According to the Secretary, Binay also asked him if he was against ceasefire and what is the opinion of Defense Chief about it.
"Sabi ko kay Binay lahat ng US ay gusto ng kapayapaan.
Sinabi niya na si Can na makipag-usap kay Misuari.
"Tigilan na raw ng tropa ng gobyerno ang pagpapaputok Only kung pigilan ng MNLF ang pagpapaputok", paliwanag ni Defense chief.
Krisis Management Council (CMC) Chairman and Zamboanga City Mayor Isabelle "Beng" Climaco Salazar, appealed to the officials of the barangay who helped security forces to defend their community.

This is also appealed to the Commander of MNLF to set all the remaining hostages and landsalt that peace for the sake of the city.
The mayor also admits that their economy is affected.
Currently, more than 62,000 have evacuated since Monday.
Other residents chose to ride a boat together with their families who are going to turn around the beach instead of crowded in evacuation centers.
