7 thousand hectares of S. Cotabato are affected by the drought.

January 4, 2016.

City of Koronadal, South Cotabato, January 4 (Pia) -- estimated 7,000 hectares of crops in South Cotabato are going to be put in danger because of the continuous drought that are caused by El Nino, Propaganda of the Polomolok and Tantangan and other small places in different parts of the province.

The fields in town of Upper Valley is not affected because there is a sufficient supply of water in the said areas of South Cotabato, according to them, the Office of the Provincial Bureau of IOS in the Bureau of Soil and Water Management Council of the Department of Agbayani, which will conduct a thorough operation in the Bureau of Soil.
