Hidilyn dagsa ang biyayang hatid ng silver medal
By Boyet Jadulco August 9, 2016 Hidilyn Diaz
Sa pagkapanalo ng silver medal sa Rio Olympics, naghihintay kay weightlifter Hidilyn Diaz ang insentibong nagkakahalaga ng P5 milyon at iba pa, ayon kay Sen. Juan Edgardo Angara.
Ipinaliwanag ni Angara, may-akda ng Republic Act No. 10699 o ang National Athletes and Coaches Benefits and Incentives Act, si Diaz ay awtomatikong bibigyan ng P5 milyon.
Ang naturang batas ang siyang nagtaas sa cash incentives at benefits ng winning athletes, coaches at trainers. Bukod dito, mabibigyan din ng scholarship at retirement benefits si Diaz na katumbas ng 25 percent ng lahat ng napanalunan sa kanyang career.
“These incentives and benefits shall be over and above the standard national athlete benefits that include discounts in lodging, transportation, medicine, sports equipment, amusement, free medical and dental consultation, PhilHealth and SSS coverage, priority in housing programs and loans, and the use of living quarters,” paliwanag ng senador.
Naghain din ng resolusyon si Angara para papurihan ang makasaysayang pagkapanalo ni Diaz sa weightlifting, kauna-unahan sa isang Filipina na nanalo ng silver medal sa Olympics.
Nasungkit ni Diaz ang medalya nang buhatin ang 200 kilogram sa women’s 53-kg category ng weightlifting. Siya rin ang unang taga-Mindanao na nakakuha ng medalya sa Olympics.
“Being first of many triumphs in Philippine sports history, Diaz’s silver medal is truly momentous. She is also the first Olympic medalist to benefit from the National Athletes and Coaches Benefits and Incentives law or Republic Act 10699 which we authored and sponsored last year.
Under the new law, she will be receiving P5 million in cash incentive tax-free. Kung nanalo siya noong 2012, P2.5 million lang ang matatanggap niya,” ayon kay Angara, vice-chairman ng Senate committee on sports.
